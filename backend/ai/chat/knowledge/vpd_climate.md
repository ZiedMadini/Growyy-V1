# VPD and Climate Control in Hydroponics

## What is VPD?

Vapour Pressure Deficit (VPD) measures the difference between the moisture in the air
and how much moisture the air can hold. It drives transpiration — high VPD pulls water
through the plant rapidly; low VPD slows transpiration and risks disease.

Formula: VPD = 0.6108 × exp(17.27 × T / (T + 237.3)) × (1 − RH/100)

## Target VPD by Stage

| Stage        | VPD (kPa) | Notes                                          |
| ------------ | --------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| Propagation  | 0.4–0.8   | Cuttings / seedlings — keep stomata open gently |
| Vegetative   | 0.8–1.2   | Promotes strong transpiration and growth       |
| Early Flower | 1.0–1.5   | Moderate stress improves resin production      |
| Late Flower  | 1.2–1.6   | Higher VPD reduces botrytis risk               |

## Fixing VPD

- VPD too HIGH (dry air): run humidifier, lower temperature, reduce airflow
- VPD too LOW (humid air): run dehumidifier, increase temperature slightly, more airflow
- Night VPD: target 0.8–1.0 kPa even at lights-off to prevent condensation

## Temperature Guidelines

| Stage        | Day Temp | Night Temp | Differential |
| ------------ | -------- | ---------- | ------------ |
| Vegetative   | 22–26°C  | 18–22°C    | 4–6°C        |
| Flowering    | 20–26°C  | 16–20°C    | 4–8°C        |
| Ripening     | 18–24°C  | 14–18°C    | 6–10°C       |

## CO₂ Enrichment

| CO₂ Level | Effect                                          |
| --------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| 400 ppm   | Ambient — baseline growth                       |
| 800 ppm   | Enhanced — standard target for indoor grows     |
| 1200 ppm  | Optimised — requires higher light intensity     |
| 1500 ppm  | Maximum useful — diminishing returns above this |
| >2000 ppm | Toxic to plants and humans                      |

CO₂ enrichment only increases yield when light (PPFD) is also above 600 µmol/m²/s.
Without adequate light, elevated CO₂ has no effect on yield.
