# Nutrient Management and Deficiency Diagnosis

## Macronutrients

### Nitrogen (N)
- Role: leaf growth, chlorophyll synthesis, protein production
- Deficiency: yellowing starting on OLD leaves (lower canopy), working up
- Excess: dark green, clawed-down leaves ("nitrogen claw"), soft vegetative growth
- In hydroponics: supplied as calcium nitrate (CaNO₃) or ammonium nitrate
- Reduce nitrogen in flowering by 30–40% to encourage fruiting

### Phosphorus (P)
- Role: root development, flower/fruit formation, energy transfer (ATP)
- Deficiency: purple/red stems and leaf undersides, slow growth; OLD leaves first
- Excess: locks out zinc, iron, manganese — causes interveinal chlorosis
- Best uptake at pH 6.0–7.0
- Increase P by 20–30% when transitioning to flowering

### Potassium (K)
- Role: stomata regulation, water uptake, sugar transport, disease resistance
- Deficiency: brown leaf edges (necrosis) starting on OLD leaves
- Excess: locks out calcium and magnesium
- Critical in late flower for dense fruit development

## Secondary Nutrients

### Calcium (Ca)
- Role: cell wall strength, prevents tip burn and blossom end rot
- Deficiency: NEW growth affected first — crinkled, distorted leaves, tip burn
- pH must be >6.0 for adequate uptake
- Cal-Mag supplement: use at 5–10 ml/10L during veg; can reduce in flower

### Magnesium (Mg)
- Role: core of chlorophyll molecule, enzyme activation
- Deficiency: interveinal chlorosis (green veins, yellow between) on OLD leaves
- Often caused by pH below 5.8 or excess potassium
- Fix: epsom salt (MgSO₄) at 1–2g/L or Cal-Mag supplement

### Sulfur (S)
- Role: protein synthesis, enzyme function
- Deficiency: young leaves turn light yellow uniformly

## Feeding Schedule Framework

### Vegetative Phase (weeks 1–4 from transplant)
- Ratio: N:P:K ≈ 3:1:2
- EC: 1.2–2.0 mS/cm
- Cal-Mag: 5–8 ml/10L
- Feed every watering

### Transition to Flower (week 1–2 of 12/12 or flip)
- Reduce N by 30%, increase P and K
- EC: 1.8–2.2 mS/cm
- Introduce bloom booster

### Flowering Phase (weeks 2–8)
- Ratio: N:P:K ≈ 1:3:2
- EC: 1.8–2.6 mS/cm
- Continue Cal-Mag at 3–5 ml/10L

### Late Flower / Ripening (final 2 weeks)
- Reduce all nutrients by 50%
- EC: 1.2–1.8 mS/cm
- Many growers stop Cal-Mag in final week

### Flush (final 1–2 weeks before harvest)
- EC: 0.0–0.4 mS/cm (plain pH'd water)
- Removes residual salts from growing medium and plant tissue
- pH: 6.0–6.2
