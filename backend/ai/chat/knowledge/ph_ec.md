# pH and EC Management in Hydroponics

## Target pH Ranges by Stage

| Stage        | pH Range  | Notes                                              |
| ------------ | --------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| Propagation  | 5.8–6.0   | Slightly acidic — promotes root development        |
| Vegetative   | 5.8–6.2   | Wider range acceptable; iron uptake optimal here   |
| Flowering    | 6.0–6.5   | Higher pH aids phosphorus and potassium uptake     |
| Flushing     | 6.0–6.2   | Neutral — wash salts without locking out nutrients |

## Critical pH Interactions

- **Below 5.5**: Calcium and Magnesium lockout — causes yellowing/tip burn
- **Above 6.8**: Iron, manganese, zinc lockout — interveinal chlorosis
- **Ideal window**: 5.8–6.3 covers uptake of all essential nutrients
- pH naturally drifts UP as plants consume nitrate (NO₃⁻) — check daily

## EC (Electrical Conductivity) Targets

| Stage        | EC (mS/cm) | TDS (ppm, 0.5 factor) |
| ------------ | ---------- | --------------------- |
| Seedling     | 0.4–0.8    | 200–400               |
| Propagation  | 0.8–1.2    | 400–600               |
| Early Veg    | 1.2–1.6    | 600–800               |
| Late Veg     | 1.6–2.0    | 800–1000              |
| Early Flower | 1.8–2.2    | 900–1100              |
| Late Flower  | 2.0–2.6    | 1000–1300             |
| Flush        | 0.0–0.4    | 0–200 (pure water)    |

## EC Correction Rules

- EC too HIGH (>target): flush with plain water, reduce nutrient dose by 20%
- EC too LOW (<target): increase nutrient solution concentration by 10–15%
- EC rising despite no dosing: evaporation — top up with plain pH'd water
- EC crashing: plant is consuming nutrients aggressively — dose per recipe

## pH Correction

- pH too HIGH: add pH Down (phosphoric or citric acid) — 1 ml/10L at a time
- pH too LOW: add pH Up (potassium hydroxide) — 0.5 ml/10L at a time
- Always adjust EC before adjusting pH
- After pH correction, wait 15 minutes and recheck before adding more
