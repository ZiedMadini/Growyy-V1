# Common Hydroponic Plant Diseases and Pests

## Fungal Diseases

### Powdery Mildew
- Appearance: white powdery coating on leaves and stems
- Cause: high humidity (>70%), poor airflow, temperature swings
- Spread: airborne spores — extremely contagious
- Prevention: keep humidity 45–60%, good circulation, no leaf crowding
- Treatment: potassium bicarbonate spray, neem oil at lights-off, remove affected leaves

### Botrytis (Grey Mould)
- Appearance: grey fuzzy mould on flowers, stems, or dead plant matter
- Cause: humidity >80% + stagnant air + dead/dying tissue
- Spread: airborne spores, explode in dense canopies
- Prevention: remove dead leaves immediately, keep RH <65% in flower
- Treatment: reduce humidity urgently, improve airflow, remove infected tissue

### Pythium (Root Rot)
- Appearance: brown, slimy, foul-smelling roots; wilting despite wet medium
- Cause: overwatering, poor oxygenation in reservoir, warm water (>22°C)
- Prevention: keep reservoir below 20°C, use air stones, don't overwater
- Treatment: beneficial bacteria (Bacillus subtilis), hydrogen peroxide flush, reduce watering

### Fusarium Wilt
- Appearance: sudden wilting, brown vascular tissue inside stems
- Cause: soil-borne fungus surviving 5–10 years in growing medium
- Prevention: sterilise equipment, use new growing medium each grow
- Treatment: no effective cure — remove plant, sterilise everything

## Bacterial Issues

### Bacterial Canker / Spot
- Appearance: dark spots with yellow halo, stem lesions
- Treatment: copper bactericide, remove infected leaves

## Pest Issues

### Spider Mites
- Appearance: tiny yellow/brown dots on leaves, webbing on undersides
- Thrive in: hot (>27°C), dry (<40% RH), stagnant air
- Treatment: predatory mites, neem oil, insecticidal soap, raise humidity
- Note: mites reproduce 50× faster than most predators — act fast

### Fungus Gnats
- Appearance: small flies near growing medium; larvae damage roots
- Cause: overwatering, organic material on surface
- Treatment: yellow sticky traps, bacillus thuringiensis israelensis (Bti), dry surface between waterings

### Whitefly
- Appearance: white flying insects on leaf undersides, sticky honeydew
- Treatment: yellow sticky traps, neem oil, insecticidal soap, introduce Encarsia wasps

### Aphids
- Appearance: clusters of green/black/white insects on new growth
- Treatment: neem oil, insecticidal soap, predatory ladybirds, high-pressure water spray

## Environmental Stress (not disease, but similar symptoms)

| Symptom                    | Likely Cause                               |
| -------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| Tip burn on new growth     | Calcium deficiency OR low VPD (poor trans) |
| Leaf curl upward           | Heat stress or overwatering                |
| Leaf curl downward (claw)  | Nitrogen excess or overwatering            |
| Purple stems               | Phosphorus deficiency or cold temps        |
| Yellow lower leaves        | Normal senescence OR nitrogen deficiency   |
| Brown crispy edges         | Potassium deficiency or salt burn          |
| Bleached/white patches     | Light burn (too close to canopy)           |
